About

We believe in writing as the process of translating thinking into language. Our competition distills the raw essence of thinking through a time limit; our rubric rewards risk-taking, nuance, and originality. We publish the winning essay each cycle to showcase both the exemplary craft and the evolution of the competition.

Mission

To cultivate individual voice through refinement, emphasizing originality, depth, and nuance.

Vision

To deepen the pursuit of excellence, challenge the conventional status quo of essay competitions, and grow into a community of individuals diverse in thought.

Rubric Evolution

We iterate with transparency; our rubric serves to empower individual voice.

2025 • Cycle 1
Refined weighting
Raised ceiling for Risk‑Taking to reward ambitious structure and argument. Clarified penalties for ornament without insight.
Characterization: This stage sharpened the competition into a higher-level intellectual arena: originality and boldness could score as highly as polish, but empty flourish would be punished.
2025 • Cycle 2
Clarity & Flow
Defined “intentional complexity” vs. “obfuscation.” Added guidance on paragraph rhythm and transitions. Characterization: Focus shifted from what was said to how it was said, rewarding not only ideas but the artistry and coherence of their delivery.
2025 • Cycle 3
Depth & Scope
Emphasized multi‑layered analysis and interdisciplinary reach. Encouraged concrete examples serving an abstract argument. Characterization: This cycle expanded ambition — pushing essays to weave the abstract and the concrete together, showing breadth without losing depth.
2025 • Cycle 4
Foundations
Established six core criteria: Clarity & Flow, Depth of Interpretation, Responsiveness to the Prompt, Profound/Interesting Ideas, Risk‑Taking, Scope. Purpose: To give structure to the competition and ensure essays are evaluated holistically rather than on surface polish alone. Characterization: A baseline rubric — broad, balanced, and designed to give writers a clear sense of direction without over-prescription.
